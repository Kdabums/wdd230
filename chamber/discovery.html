Lekki Ikoyi Link Bridge


The Lekki-Ikoyi Link Bridge is a 1.36 km cable-stayed bridge in Lagos State. It links the Phase 1 area of Lekki, with Ikoyi district of Lagos. The bridge was commissioned on 29 May 2013 by the Governor of Lagos State, Babatunde Raji Fashola.

Afrika Shrine


Located at Ikeja, the shrine is definitely one of the fun spots to visit in Lagos and is currently the host location of the Felabration music festival, an event held yearly in honor of the musical legend, Fela Anikulapo Kuti. It displays photo galleries of Fela and there are musical performances from different musicians especially Femi and Seun Kuti.

Lekki Conservation Centre (LCC)


The Lekki Conservation centre is a 78-hectare (190-acre) natural resource conservation in Lekki, Lagos state. You get to immerse yourself in the beauties of nature on the amazing tour of this centre. You can even climb above tree canopies on the longest canopy bridge walk in Africa. The canopy walk is about 401-metre long. It is an engineering feat and an amazing, suspended swinging bridge walkway. It's an untouched natural vegetation that houses various plant and animal species.

National Theather Nigeria


Constructed in 1976, National Arts Theatre is a multi-purpose national monument for the preservation, presentation and promotion of Nigerian arts and culture. Located in Iganmu on the mainland area of Lagos, the theatre which was constructed during the military regime of Gen. Olusegun Obasanjo, is the primary centre for performing arts in Nigeria. Its exterior is shaped like a military hat making it stands out as a landmark edifice within the city.

Nike Art Gallery


Nike Art Gallery is an art gallery in Lagos and Osun state owned by Nike Davies-Okundaye. The gallery is one of the largest of its kind in West Africa, it boasts a collection of about 8,000 diverse artworks of different descriptions such as paintings in different media, batik and Adire fabrics, embroidery, appliqué, beadworks, pen & ink drawing on paper and on canvas, aluminum & copper works and sculptures also in different media, all set out in the most conducive climatic condition from various Nigerian artists.
